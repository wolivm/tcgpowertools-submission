## Captions

YouTube's auto-captions are decent for native English, but they consistently mishear product names. Always proof and correct:

- "PowerTools" (one word, capital P and T) — auto-captions often render this as "power tools"
- "Cardmarket" — sometimes captioned as "card market"
- "Cardtrader" — sometimes captioned as "card trader"

Cleanest workflow: upload the final script as a `.srt` (sync to your recorded timing). YouTube Studio's caption editor lets you paste plain text and auto-sync.

## Pacing & post-production notes

- **Zoom-ins:** add a tighter zoom on each mode tile as you name it. ScreenStudio's click-following handles most of this; bump the zoom level slightly tighter for "Bulk" and "Import a File" since those tiles sit lower on the screen.
- **Beats:** add a ~0.3s pause before each mode name appears on screen. Gives the viewer's eye time to track.
- **Music:** subtle background bed, ducked under the voice (-22 LUFS). Royalty-free options: Epidemic Sound "Soft Tech" or Artlist's productivity tracks.
- **Transitions:** a single-frame flash between modes feels punchy without being distracting. Avoid spinning/sliding transitions — they age badly.
- **Cold open:** start straight on the dashboard, no animated intro. The audience is busy and goal-oriented; a logo sting is friction.
- **End frame:** hold on the dashboard for ~1 second before fading to the end screen so YouTube doesn't crop your last word.
